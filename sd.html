Sustainable Development

What is Sustainable Development and why is it important?

Under the pillar of Sustainable Development, Eccopoli adheres to the seventeen Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) of the United Nations. The SDGs have been built over decades of work by member states and the United Nations, including the Department of Social and Economic Affairs.

In June 1992, at the Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, more than 178 countries adopted Agenda 21, a comprehensive action plan to create a global Sustainable Development partnership to improve human lives and protect the environment.

    • Member states unanimously adopted the Millennium Declaration at the Millennium Summit in September 2000 at the United Nations headquarters in New York. The summit led to the development of eight Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) to reduce extreme poverty by 2015.

    • The Johannesburg Declaration on Sustainable Development and the Implementation Plan, adopted at the World Summit on Sustainable Development in South Africa in 2002, reaffirmed the commitments of the global community to the eradication of poverty and the environment, and built on Agenda-21 and the Millennium Declaration by including more emphasis on multilateral collaborations.

    • At the United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development (Rio 20) in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in June 2012, member states adopted the resulting document, “The Future We Want,” in which they decided, inter alia, to launch a process to develop a set of SDGs to build on the MDGs and to establish the UN High Level Political Forum on Sustainable Development.

    • The outcome of Rio 20 also contained other measures to implement sustainable development, including mandates for future work programs in development financing, small island developing states and more.

    • In 2013, the General Assembly organized a 30-member Open Working Group to develop a proposal on the SDGs.

    • In January 2015, the General Assembly began the negotiation process on the post-2015 development agenda. The process culminated in the subsequent adoption of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, with 17 Sustainable Development Goals at its center, at the United Nations Summit for Sustainable Development in September 2015.

Today, the Division for Sustainable Development Goals (DSDGs) in the United Nations Department for Economic and Social Affairs (UNDESA) provides substantive support and capacity building on the SDGs and their related thematic issues, including water, energy, climate, oceans, urbanization, transport, technology, the Global Development Report (GSDR), collaborations and Small Island Developing States. DSDG plays a key role in evaluating the systematic implementation of the United Nations 2030 Agenda and on advocacy and outreach activities related to the SDGs. In order to make the 2030 Agenda a reality, broad ownership of the SDGs must translate into a strong commitment on the part of all actors to implement the global objectives. DSDG focuses on helping to facilitate this commitment.

Sustainable Development for Eccopoli

Eccopoli intends to carry out high impact projects at the community level to improve the lives of community members where it is most needed.

TIn the northeast of Haiti, not all of the thirteen cities in the department have yet 24-hour access to electricity. Many of these cities do not have access to potable water either. Sometimes the population does not have enough resources or support from the state to repair their schools. Children's benches in schools are not enough and sometimes rainwater seeps through the roofs. It is common to see children walking with their little broom chairs from home to school, because in schools there are not enough chairs. The bathrooms and toilets for children in schools are also, in general, in poor condition.

Eccopoli enters these communities and identifies the most needed projects through community meetings. Using different types of financing and donation mechanisms, Eccopoli seeks to execute projects to improve the situation in a sustainable way at the community level.
