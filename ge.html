Gender Equality

Today in Haiti and Latin America there are exuberant percentages of human trafficking, domestic abuse, prostitution, and violence against women. In its Global Trafficking of Persons Report 2018, UNODC gives us accounts on the trafficking of persons.

Globally, countries are detecting and reporting more victims of trafficking. In 2016, the number of victims reported globally reached its highest level when more than 24,000 were detected. The increase in the number of victims detected has been more pronounced in the Americas.

The report shows that 72% of victims detected in 2018 worldwide are female. 49% of cases are adult women and 23% are girls, which represents an increase in relation to the previous report of 2016.

The majority of victims detected worldwide are trafficked for the purpose of sexual exploitation, although this pattern is not uniform in all regions. In Central America and the Caribbean, more girls are trafficked for sexual exploitation...

Gender Equality for Eccopoli

Eccopoli brings to the community level awareness and consciousness to avoid any type of violence against women, to promote social equity between girls and boys, women and men, and senior citizens with their respective gender.

Through conferences to promote the eradication of violence, trafficking, domestic abuse of women and girls, Eccopoli tries to act as a social catalyst. Through mediation programs, Eccopoli is integrated at the community level to mediate in the resolution of conflicts between members of different genders to reach viable agreements. Eccopoli also provides support to victims of trafficking, victims of domestic violence and social inequality. Eccopoli collaborates with the National Human Rights Commission denouncing any abuse or offense to women's human rights.
